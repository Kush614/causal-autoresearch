OUR METHOD

Three Causal Tests,
One Confidence Score

Pearl's do-calculus applied to discrete program optimization

Test A: Ablation
Weight: 50%
Reverse the agent's patch. Re-run training. If val_bpb worsens without the change, it was causal.
Test B: Replication
Weight: 30%
Run 3x with seeds [42, 137, 999]. Low variance = seed-independent = causal.
Test C: Transfer
Weight: 20%
Re-run and compare to baseline. Consistent improvement = generalizable = causal.

score = 0.5 * ablation + 0.3 * replication + 0.2 * transfer   |   >0.8 strongly causal   >0.5 likely   <0.2 spurious
